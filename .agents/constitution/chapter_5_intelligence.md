# Paradise OS Constitution v1.0

## Chapter 5: Intelligence Constitution

### 5.1 Competitive Intelligence
1. **Dynamic Entities:** Competitors must not be tracked as static names on a spreadsheet. They are dynamic networks. Paradise OS must reverse-engineer their formulations, track their SKUs, and monitor historical price fluctuations.
2. **Claim Auditing:** Every marketing claim made by a competitor must be logged, sourced, and systematically cross-referenced against empirical evidence to verify its legitimacy.
3. **Supply Chain Mapping:** The system must relentlessly attempt to map competitors to their ultimate raw material suppliers utilizing import data, certifications, and trade show intelligence.

### 5.2 Supplier Intelligence
1. **Vetting over Volume:** Supplier intelligence prioritizes deep vetting. The system must track a supplier's total capabilities, verified certifications (USDA Organic, ISO, GMP), and historical reliability.
2. **Network Mapping:** A supplier profile must explicitly list every known brand, product, and importer that relies on them, illuminating the hidden dependencies within the market.

### 5.3 Importer Intelligence
1. **Importers as CRM Entities:** Importers and distributors are not merely references on a bill of lading; they are first-class CRM entities within Paradise OS. 
2. **Deep Tracking:** The system must track their country of origin, buyer types, annual volumes, the certifications they require, their existing supplier networks, and all active negotiations or RFQs (Requests for Quotation).
3. **Uncovering the Middlemen:** Importer intelligence exists to strip away the obfuscation of the global supply chain, allowing Paradise Organics to see exactly who moves the materials.

### 5.4 Laboratory Intelligence
1. **Anchoring Claims to Science:** Product and ingredient claims are meaningless without scientific validation. Laboratory intelligence anchors the system to reality.
2. **COA Parsing:** The system must store and parse Certificates of Analysis (COAs), tracking specific metrics like purity percentages, heavy metal content, and microbiological results.
3. **Accreditation Tracking:** The reliability of a lab report is entirely dependent on the lab issuing it. The OS must track the accreditation status and historical reliability of the third-party laboratories themselves.

### 5.5 Market Intelligence
1. **Macro Synthesis:** Market intelligence synthesizes broad industry trends, regulatory changes, and consumer sentiment shifts.
2. **Micro Application:** Macro trends must be explicitly connected to specific SKUs, ingredients, or regions. For example, a regulatory change in a specific country must flag all suppliers and products associated with that region within the OS.
